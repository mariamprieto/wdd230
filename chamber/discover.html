Geoeconomic Structure of Venezuela

Inflation in Venezuela

According to information from Statista, Venezuela will be number one on the list of countries with the highest inflation for this year.Inflation in Venezuela accelerated and reached 10.1% in May, which represents an increase of 6.5 points compared to April, when it stood at 3.6%.

The items that increased the most the previous month were education, with 54.9%; followed by goods and services, with 24.8%; clothing and footwear, 18.6%; recreation, with an increase of 16%; housing rental, which rose by 13.2%; and food, which grew by 11.9%.

Primary Sector or Agricultural Sector

The primary sector, also known as agriculture, is the one that obtains the product from natural resources (both renewable and non-renewable) to later transform it into unprocessed raw materials destined mainly for industrial production and the supply of certain types of services.

Some examples of activities in the primary sector are the following: Agriculture, Livestock, Fishing, Exploitation of forest resources and Mining.

Secondary Sector or Industrial Sector.

The secondary or industrial sector includes economic activities aimed at the transformation of raw materials, that is, industry and construction. Thus, while the primary sector is limited to directly obtaining the resources of nature, the secondary sector executes industrial procedures to transform said resources. This transformation activity can also be artisanal, when the production of the goods is carried out in a simple way and involves print runs of few units

Examples of the secondary sector include: Metal processing and foundry, Automotive production, Textile production, Chemical industry, Manufacturing, Energy and engineering.

Tertiary Sector or Service Sector.

The tertiary sector is made up of all those activities that do not produce merchandise, but supply the market with services and goods, for this reason it is also called the service sector. Today, this sector has diversified and grown spectacularly, accounting for up to 70% of the gross domestic product of some developed countries. Examples of the tertiary sector would be the following: Transport, Distribution, Tourism, Entertainment, Finance and Public Administrations
